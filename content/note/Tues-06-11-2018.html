Mission & vission, objectives, key results, and tasks.

So today we continue trying to understand all the rpart() function and algorithm.

In response to one of yesterday’s questions, the PRESS statistic is the predicted residual error sum of squares:

\(\sum_{i=1}^{n} (y_i-\hat{y}_{i,-i})^2\)

According to trusty Wikipedia, the PRESS statistic is a form of cross validation derived from comparing the model’s predictions vs. actual values on a dataset that was not used for fitting the model.

A model that is overfitted would give small residuals using the observations with which the model was fitted and larger residuals with observations that were not used to fit the model. This makes sense.

Now that I understand the PRESS statistic which is called xerror in the rpart output table. I still didn’t understand the xstd. I assumed it was the standard deviation of the xerror but after some manual attempts at calculating xstd based on xerror and having no luck. I took to Cross Validated to sort me out. According to user bearoak on this thread, it is not the standard deviation as one would think because of the std bit of xstd, but it is actually the standard error which is the standard deviation divided by the number of observations.
